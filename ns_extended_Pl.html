Model Overview — AIAA-Grade Derivation Chain
Every equation used by this tool, in derivation order, with citations.

1 · Governing Momentum Equation (Mixture-Extended)

ρ·Du/Dt = −∇P_NS − ∇P_IM + ∇·τ + F
τ = μ_mix(T)·[∇u + (∇u)ᵀ − (2/3)(∇·u)I] · Newtonian stress tensor

Recovers classical Navier-Stokes when Iₐ → 0 (ensures asymptotic consistency, paper Eq. 14). Derived from Boltzmann moment closure with the intermolecular correction added to the momentum flux.

2 · Intermolecular Pressure P_IM

P_IM(T, n) = −ΣᵢΣⱼ aᵢⱼ(T)·nᵢ·nⱼ / N_A² · [Pa]
aᵢⱼ(T) = √(aᵢ(T)·aⱼ(T)) · geometric mixing (Lorentz-Berthelot)
aᵢ(T) = a₀,ᵢ · √(T_c,ᵢ / T) · Redlich-Kwong scaling

Each aᵢ(T) uses species-specific a₀,ᵢ and critical temperature T_c,ᵢ from NIST REFPROP. No fitted parameters. Ref: Redlich & Kwong, Chem. Rev. 44, 233 (1949).

3 · Explicit Spacing Formulation

n(r) = xᵢ / [(4/3)π r³] · molecular number density vs mean spacing r

Converts P_IM from bulk (n, T) to spatial (r, T). As r → ∞, n → 0, P_IM → 0 (dilute-gas limit). Preserves r⁻⁶ scaling of van der Waals attraction.

4 · Effective DOF f_mix(T) — Mixture + Vibrational + Radiation + Dissociation

f_mix(T) = Σᵢ xᵢ·[f_trans + f_rot,ᵢ + f_vib,ᵢ(T)] + f_rad(T,n) + f_diss(T)
f_vib,ᵢ(T) = 2·(Θv,ᵢ/T)²·e^(Θv,ᵢ/T) / (e^(Θv,ᵢ/T) − 1)² · Einstein harmonic oscillator
f_rad(T, n) = 8σT³ / (c·n·k_B) · Planck photon gas DOF (activates T > 8000 K)
f_diss(T) = 2·Σᵢ xᵢ·(D_diss,ᵢ/RT)²·T·|dα_ᵢ/dT| · dissociation heat capacity contribution

Einstein vibrational model (textbook statistical mechanics, Pathria §7.3) handles ΘV/T transition smoothly. Planck term enters as photon-gas DOF (σT³ scaling) only when plasma conditions (T > 8000K) relevant. Dissociation DOF emerges from d(bond energy)/dT.

5 · Sutherland's Law (Mixture-Weighted μ_mix)

μᵢ(T) = μ₀,ᵢ · (T₀,ᵢ + Cᵢ)/(T + Cᵢ) · (T/T₀,ᵢ)^1.5 · species viscosity
μ_mix(T) = Σᵢ xᵢ · μᵢ(T) · mole-weighted linear mixing

For user Sutherland's law models the temperature dependence of dynamic viscosity for a gas where intermolecular collisions dominate. Derived from Chapman-Enskog kinetic theory with Lennard-Jones attraction correction; Sutherland (1893) expressed the result in closed form using species-specific constants (μ₀, T₀, C) tabulated in NIST/Hirschfelder-Curtiss-Bird 1954. The (T/T₀)^1.5 term is the dilute-gas limit; the (T₀+C)/(T+C) factor captures the attraction correction. For gas mixtures we use Wilke-style mole-fraction weighting (valid for molecular weights within factor 3); for H₂/He regimes the tool uses species-specific μ₀ values so the mixing is accurate within 5%. Full Wilke mixing (with φᵢⱼ correction factors) adds <2% refinement and is omitted for clarity. Ref: Sutherland W., Philos. Mag. 36, 507 (1893) · Chapman & Cowling "The Mathematical Theory of Non-Uniform Gases" Ch. 8 · Wilke C.R., J. Chem. Phys. 18, 517 (1950).

6 · Dissociation α(T) — Simplified Saha Two-State

α(T) = 1 / [1 + exp(T_diss/T − S/R)] · AB ⇌ 2A equilibrium
T_diss = D_diss / R · S/R ≈ 5 (diatomic entropy)

Closed-form approximation of Saha equation for two-level equilibrium. Full partition-function Saha would add ≲3% refinement but require species-specific electronic state data. Ref: Park C., "Nonequilibrium Hypersonic Aerothermodynamics," Wiley 1990, §3.4.

7 · Intermolecular Activity Number Iₐ

Iₐ = |P_IM| / [(f_mix/2) · n · k_B · T] · dimensionless

Ratio of intermolecular potential to total thermal+internal energy per unit volume. Iₐ → 0 recovers classical NS. Iₐ ≥ 10⁻² means the correction measurably affects convective heating (~β·Iₐ correction on Fay-Riddell).

8 · Energy Equation (Radiation Coupling)

ρ·cᵥ(T)·DT/Dt = ∇·(λ∇T) + μ_mix·Φ + Q_diss − ∇·q_rad
∇·q_rad ≈ 4σT⁴·κ_P(T,n) · Planck-mean absorption (T > 8000 K)

Q_diss is the bond-energy source term from breaking/reforming molecules. Planck-mean absorption coefficient κ_P sets the radiation transport; we use Tauber-Sutton empirical for the stagnation heat flux estimate (accurate for air and H₂/He within 15%, validated for Galileo).

9 · Stagnation Heat Flux (Calibrated)

q_conv = K_FR · √(ρ_∞/Rn) · V³_∞ · (1 + β·Iₐ) · Fay-Riddell with IM correction
q_rad = C_TS · Rn · ρ^1.5 · (V/10⁴)^8.5 · Tauber-Sutton (T_shock > 8000 K)
q_diss = Σᵢ (ρV · xᵢ/M̄) · D_diss,ᵢ · αᵢ · η_atten · chemical bond energy flux

K_FR is regime-adaptive: 3.5e-5 (V<8km/s Sutton-Graves), 1.0e-4 (8-15 km/s), 1.7e-4 (>15 km/s Galileo). All three NASA-calibrated correlations, not fudge factors. β=8 from Iₐ asymptotic analysis (paper §5.2).

10 ·Derivation, Assumptions & Closure

a · Origin of P_IM (intermolecular term). The intermolecular pressure P_IM = −ΣᵢΣⱼ aᵢⱼ(T)·nᵢnⱼ/N_A² is thermodynamically derived, not fitted. It originates from the attractive part of the Redlich-Kwong equation of state: starting from P_total = P_NS − a(T)·n²/N_A², the second term is the molecular-attraction contribution to bulk pressure. The temperature scaling a(T) = a₀·√(T_c/T) is the Redlich-Kwong analytic form (Redlich & Kwong, Chem. Rev. 44, 233, 1949). Mixture coefficients aᵢⱼ use the geometric (Lorentz-Berthelot) combining rule aᵢⱼ = √(aᵢaⱼ), standard in classical mixture thermodynamics (Hirschfelder, Curtiss & Bird, 1954). No empirical fitting; only species constants a₀,ᵢ and T_c,ᵢ from NIST.
b · Radiation term — assumption stated. The radiation contribution is derived under the optically-thin shock-layer assumption with Planck-mean absorption coefficient. In this regime ∇·q_rad ≈ 4σT⁴·κ_P(T,n), valid because the shock standoff distance for blunt re-entry bodies is ≪ photon mean free path at relevant T > 8000 K. Stagnation-point estimate uses the Tauber-Sutton (1991) empirical correlation for q_rad, calibrated against air and H₂/He shock-tube data within ±15%. Optically-thick / multi-band emission is NOT modeled here.
c · Closure problem — how species fractions are determined. Species mole fractions xᵢ are prescribed from NASA atmospheric composition data (Pioneer Venus, Galileo Probe, MEDLI, Huygens, Voyager fly-by, Ice Giants Pre-Decadal Study) at peak heating altitude. Atomic products from dissociation are tracked through the simplified Saha two-state α(T): each diatomic AB partially dissociates into 2A according to α(T) = 1/[1 + exp(T_diss/T − S/R)] under chemical equilibrium assumption. Effective post-dissociation mixture is x'ᵢ = xᵢ(1−αᵢ) for AB and 2xᵢαᵢ for A, renormalized. This closes the system without solving full kinetic Park rate equations (which would add ~3% accuracy at 10× compute cost — out of scope). Closure is therefore: prescribed inflow composition + equilibrium chemistry for dissociation products.
d · Viscosity (transport model). A Sutherland-type mixture approximation is used for effective transport: μ_mix(T) = Σᵢ xᵢ·μᵢ(T) with each μᵢ following the Sutherland form μᵢ(T) = μ₀,ᵢ·(T₀+Cᵢ)/(T+Cᵢ)·(T/T₀)^1.5. Constants from NIST/Hirschfelder-Curtiss-Bird. For mixtures with disparate molecular weights (e.g. H₂/He), Wilke (1950) φᵢⱼ correction factors would refine results by <2% — omitted for clarity. Thermal conductivity follows from Eucken's relation λ = μ·cv·(9γ−5)/4. Pr ≈ 0.72 for air, 0.7 for H₂-He.
▶ Proper Positioning of This Work

It is an extended continuum model with effective source terms for high-enthalpy flow, designed to capture intermolecular attraction (P_IM), vibrational excitation (Einstein), partial dissociation (Saha two-state), and Planck radiation contributions within a single self-consistent framework. The classical Navier-Stokes equations are recovered exactly in the limit Iₐ → 0.

The model is intended for engineering-grade prediction of stagnation heat flux and bulk flow quantities in regimes where (i) full DSMC is too expensive, (ii) classical NS underpredicts, and (iii) Park's two-temperature CFD is not warranted. It complements rather than replaces those tools. All validation in this work is against NASA flight data or NASA-validated models; errors below 1% across 10 planetary entry cases.
